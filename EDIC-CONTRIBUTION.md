# EDIC contribution — K0NSULT as candidate contributor to a Digital Commons EDIC

**Version:** 1.0 · **License of this document:** Apache-2.0 · **Status:** positioning statement,
candidate declaration. **Not** a membership application and **not** a stewardship claim.

Answers COM(2026)503 needs **#40-42** (a European Digital Infrastructure Consortium — "Digital
Commons EDIC" — as a durable vehicle to steward and co-fund reusable open building blocks).
This document states, honestly and narrowly, **what K0NSULT would contribute** to such a
consortium and **what K0NSULT is not**.

> **Doctrine (invariant):** `claim ≤ proof` · engine hidden · agents-not-people ·
> reputation soulbound (not crypto) · Human Override Always · No Password Custody.

## 1. Position in one sentence
K0NSULT is a bootstrapped SME offering a **small polyrepo of evidence-first building blocks** and
a **copyable micro-OSPO governance pattern** as a candidate contributor to a Digital Commons EDIC —
**not** as a founding member, host, treasurer, or steward-of-others' code.

The distinctive contribution is not any single repo — it is the **reproducible eligibility bar**:
a demonstration that a single SME can make a module credibly commons-ready (OSI licence + patent
grant + re-derivable SBOM + scoped NOTICE + evidence classes) **without first founding a foundation**.
This directly informs how an EDIC could admit small contributors (needs #23/#24/#32).

## 3. What K0NSULT explicitly is NOT (boundaries)
- **Not a steward of others' code.** We steward only our own polyrepo. We do not offer to host,
  govern, or gate-keep third-party projects inside an EDIC.
- **Not a foundation or its replacement.** The charter's graduation path assumes a *neutral*
  European foundation exists; K0NSULT is a contributor to it, never a substitute for it.
- **Not an infrastructure host / network operator.** No ledger, no token, no coin, no network
  liquidity. Reputation is soulbound and non-transferable.
- **Not a treasurer.** We do not seek to administer EDIC funds; we seek eligibility for our
  building blocks to be endorsed/co-funded/procured.
- **Not a personal-data processor in the commons.** No commons repo scores natural persons;
  subjects are software agents (closed enum + negative test). PII stays out of every catalogued repo.
- **Not a proprietary-engine vendor inside the commons.** The `k0nsult.cloud` engine is out of
  scope by construction and is never contributed, catalogued, or merged into a commons repo.

## 4. What K0NSULT would ask of the EDIC (reciprocal need)
1. Recognise the **micro-OSPO / solo-maintainer track** — qualifying artefact = signed SBOM,
   not foundation membership (#32).
2. Fund a **sovereign EU keyless-signing** capability so maintainers under No Password Custody can
   attest their SBOM/provenance (the CRA art.25 blind spot). Today our signing is an explicit **GAP**.
3. Provide the **neutral foundation graduation target** the charter's exit path assumes (#25-28),
   so an SME-stewarded repo can transfer without relicensing.
4. Adopt **agents-not-people / no-personal-data-on-ledger** as an EDIC eligibility criterion,
   enforced in code, not policy prose.

## 5. Readiness of this candidacy (claim ≤ proof)
- **DOWÓD:** the governance charter, the eligibility model, and the evidence-first validator are
  shipped and re-derivable today.
- **GAP:** several building blocks are roadmap-stage; public repositories are prepared locally and
  remain private pending operator sign-off (publication is irreversible — Human Override Always).
- **GAP:** no sovereign signing key exists (No Password Custody). Signed releases await either an
  operator-held key or the EU keyless capability requested in §4.2.
- This document is a **candidate declaration**, not an accepted membership. Any submission to an
  EDIC process is made by the human operator; no agent submits, signs, or commits on their behalf.
